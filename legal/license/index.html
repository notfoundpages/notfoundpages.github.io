Licensing FAQ

Common questions about using our code in your projects.

Do I have to open-source my project if I use these pages?
No. The MIT License is not a "copyleft" license (unlike the GPL). You can use our open-source 404 pages inside your proprietary, closed-source web applications without being required to share your own source code.
Can I sell a website template that includes a notfoundpages design?
Yes. You are allowed to include our designs in commercial themes, SaaS boilerplates, or templates that you sell, provided you leave the MIT attribution comment in the source code of the file.
If I submit a Pull Request, do I lose ownership of my design?
No. You retain the original copyright to your specific contribution. However, by submitting the PR to our organisation, you irrevocably agree to distribute your work under the project's MIT License so that others can use it freely.
Can I use the notfoundpages logo on my site?
You may use our logo to link back to us or mention that you use our resources. However, you cannot use our logo, name, or branding to imply that we officially endorse your company, product, or service.
